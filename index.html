CAMERON, Christopher JF

PhD, McGill University
Chicago, IL

I am a computational biologist completing a PhD in Computer Science (with a concentration in Bioinformatics) at McGill University (Montréal, QC, Canada). My PhD dissertation focuses on 3D regulatory genomics – in particular, a family of biochemical techniques (Chromosome Conformation Capture [3C]) and the application of bioinformatics approaches, statistical modeling, and machine learning to biochemical/genomic data at high resolution. Under the supervision of Drs. Mathieu Blanchette and Josée Dostie, I successfully defended my PhD dissertation. I am now transitioning to a postdoctoral position at Yale University (New Haven, CT, USA) with Drs. Mark Gerstein and Yong Xiong.

If you’re interested in collaborating with me, please contact me as I am always happy to talk science over a beer.
