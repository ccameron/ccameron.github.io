Christopher John Frederick Cameron, PhD

1 Department of Molecular Biophysics and Biochemistry, Yale University
2 Department of Radiology and Biomedical Imaging, Yale University

As a computational biologist, I develop novel statistical, machine learning, and bioinformatics approaches to emerging molecular biology technologies. My postdoctoral research focuses on cryogenic Electron Microscopy (cryo-EM) image processing, specifically addressing the modeling and integration of prior information. During my PhD, I implemented analyses of Chromosome Conformation Capture (3C) and Chromatin ImmunoPrecipitation (ChIP) data that provided insight into the regulation of genomes through the organization of chromatin’s higher-order structures.

Keywords: bioinformatics, AI specialist, 4D regulatory genomics, structural biology
